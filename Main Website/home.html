About Me

“Every morning, the sun rises with endless possibilities that give you endless opportunities to begin again and to create a brand-new life.” - Debasisha Mridha

I am a junior at the University of Washington studying Applied Computational Math Science (ACMS) with a focus in data science and numerical algorithms. While studying, I am apart of many other organizations including Purple Caster Minions, where I facilitate different events for our members, the Vietnamese Eucharistic Youth Movement, where I teach about Catholic teachings to students ranging from ages thirteen to eighteen, and Phi Chi Theta, where I coordinate fundraising campaigns.

Aside from that, I also work at the University of Washington's college of education as an IT support user, conduct research with UW's college of business, and teach code at UW's Husky Union Building.

With everything going on though, I still make some time out to read, work on development projects, and learn on my own. Every day, there's always something new to learn and a new challenge in front of yourself, and I always aim to push through that challenge to face another greater one. Challenge after challenge, you eventually reach a point where you never imagined you could be, and I want to continue exploring that through the end of my college experience and onward.

Always Learning

"Leadership and learning are indispensable to each other. - John F. Kennedy"

Starting June of 2019, that would be the first time I would classify myself as the student I am today. A student, who does not only learn to reach an end goal, but instead continues to look onward without limitation to discover more.

Through learning to only discover more, there's a transitional point that happened as school work didn't seem like work, but it was more of a way to continue discovering yourself and who wouldn't want that haha. Not to mention, the possibilities started to become endless of what learning could lead you to do next.

In the future, I hope to use all of the knowledge I accumulate today to start a business in the medical industry. Through growing up, I was always advised to become a doctor, unsure if whether it was because of the money or the chance to help others. What sticked with me the most though was the latter. I continued to ask myself how I can help others every single day, and although I can't help through medical synopsis, I hope to empower those who can and provide more tools for people to acquire healthcare.

Coding Opens Doors

"Give a man a program, frustrate him for a day. Teach a man to program, frustrate him for a lifetime."

Within university, there are countless number of pathways you can choose. "Maybe I'll take organic chemistry or economics," I thought to myself, but for some reason, I was always against taking a computer science class. I never looked into computer science, but I always had this stigma of just sitting at a desk to do work, and I loathed it because how could I make an impact on others that way?

This same thought persisted up until November of 2017, where someone challenged my mindset as I was signing up for classes. "Just try it out and see how the class goes. You will never know what it is like until you try," he said. After careful consideration, I then took the leap of faith and took the computer science class. Soon enough, my eyes were opened up to a new world of possibilities about our vast society today, and I could not stop learning more about the capabilities of computer science.

A few years passed from learning my first print statement in java, and since then I have assisted many research projects, developed vast programs, and built data insight for companies. While signing up for those classes, I never thought I would be making these discoveries and be where I am at today, but the future can surprise you in countless ways. The journey is still young, and as I continue to journey through self-learning more about the different capabilities of computer science, I hope to continue pushing the limits of this industry.

Stepping Away from Tech
