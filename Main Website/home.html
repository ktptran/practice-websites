The story of Kevin Thinh-Phu Tran

"The man that embodies rise and grind" - Daniel Pham

I am a 21 year old junior at the University of Washington who has been diving head first into the computer and data science field for the past two years.

My passion is all about continuing to learn something new every day to both challenge myself and to thank those who have shaped me into who I am today. Always learning has led me to not only explore new things, but to invoke new identities within myself as an ongoing student, developer, and a reader.

I also am an avid leader who always pushes the threshold of any new situation. From the words of one of my colleagues, I am "the first to notice areas that need improvement and take initiative to spearhead those changes," and I hope to continue doing this with every new opportunity.

I am currently looking for opportunities to connect with others to talk about industry, literature, and learning new perspectives. Feel free to reach out to me through any of the platforms, and I look forward to what comes next in our conversations.

Always Learning

“Every morning, the sun rises with endless possibilities that give you endless opportunities to begin again and to create a brand-new life.” - Debasisha Mridha

Going through college, I jumped around many different fields including chemistry, business, engineering, and proof-based mathematics. All of them had something unique, but I never felt the urge to keep learning more outside of classwork. This was the case up until early 2019 where I stumbled on online videos and began self-learning.

This would be the first time I would consider myself a student as I am hunting to learn more instead of being gifted it. The start of doing this was always the hardest part, but after continually pushing myself this past year, the rewards continue to push me to search more.

As I enter into my final undergraduate year, I aim to continue self-learning to build the foundation of always learning after graduating with a bachelors of science in applied math.

Coding Opens Doors

"Give a man a program, frustrate him for a day. Teach a man to program, frustrate him for a lifetime." - Muhammad Waseem

Learning computer science and programming the past two years have continually opened my eyes to how amazing this world is. Using the programs themselves are amazing, but as you delve deeper into the field you experience how computers talk throughout the globe, how information is processed, and more!

As a developer now, I am continually challenging myself to learn these new concepts and translate them to new projects. Not only will it build my knowledge, but I hope to translate these fundamentals to build a startup. As a developer, I aim to not only build the program to make it, but to have a vision of changing the world with every functionality created.

What Books Bestow

“A reader lives a thousand lives before he dies . . . The man who never reads lives only one.” – George R.R. Martin

A few years ago, I traveled to different states alone to learn more about myself and the world. Those moments were very enriching to learn how to survive and experience new adventures, but there was something missing. I never determined what until I started reading my first book in August of 2019 called "The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck".

The book was highly informative about life struggles and opened a new vantage point of approaching life. Unlike traveling, you are not able to see amazing vantage points, but you are able to experience life through the eyes of someone else and read about their methodology to enrich your own.

From that point on, I have read around twenty books focused on concepts like self help, medical stances, and more! I hope to share these books out eventually in a blog in the coming future, but for now, here is the list of books, I have read and would recommend as I hope it enriches your life just as it did my own.
